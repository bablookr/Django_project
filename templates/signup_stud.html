About Us

The Department of English was established with the inception of the Banaras Hindu University in 1916. It is a large Department in the university. 'Indian Literature in English and Post Colonial Literatures' are the focal areas of research and training. The Department has been publishing regularly a research journal 'Criticism and Research' since 1964. At the postgraduate level in the final semester, optional papers such as Indian Literature in Translation, New Literatures in English, Women Writing, and European Literature in Translation are offered to the students. The Department organizes three annual memorial lectures - The Nag, Purohit Swami and Vikramaditya Rai Memorial Lectures. Eminent scholars are invited to deliver these memorial lectures on various modern aspects of English literature. The Department organizes seminars regularly.
The Department of English, one of the major department of Banaras Hindu University, came into existence with the very inception of this seat of learning in 1916. The formative years of the Department were shaped by a galaxy of scholars like Joygopal Banerjee, Ronald Nixon, P. Sheshadry and U.C. Nag among others. Its scholastic traditions were farther strengthened by C. N Menon, B. L. Sahney. R. A. Diwedi, N M Kulkarni, V Rai. T. N. Singh, U. P. Mathur, S. M. Pandeya, G.B. Mohan Thampi, and R R Mehrotra,who had distinguished themselves in various spheres of English students.

Richards, Somerset Maugham, Stephen Spender William Golding, Angus Wilson. Margaret Drabble, Norman Holand, Bruce King, Michael Horlando Michel Pousse and Michael Franklin.
